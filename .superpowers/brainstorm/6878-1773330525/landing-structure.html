What's the primary goal of this redesign?

The current page is minimal — a short hero and a form. Understanding the priority shapes everything about layout and content.

A

Show the output first

Lead with big, beautiful generated book pages — spreads, covers, illustration grids. Let parents see exactly what they'll get before they fill in a single field. Build desire through evidence.

B

Reduce time-to-book

Keep the form front and center but make the page feel more polished and trustworthy. Less persuasion, more momentum — get the parent into the flow as fast as possible.

C

Both — tiered layout

A scrollable page: wow-moment hero with book screenshots at the top, then the form below the fold. Parents who need convincing scroll down; parents ready to act get the form quickly.

Context: the CL-009b plan specifically mentions "generated book screenshots as hero images" — Option A and C both honour that intent. A keeps the form below the fold; C keeps both visible above it.
